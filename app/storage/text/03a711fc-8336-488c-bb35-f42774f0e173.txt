Modern History Important One Liners
After researching the High Court syllabus and previous year papers of other examinations, we
found that most of the questions are asked from these topics-
1. Indian National Movement ( 1885-1947)
 Gandhi Era ( 1915-1947): This is the most asked topic.
o Champaran , Kheda , Ahmedabad mill strike.
o Non-cooperation movement , Khilafat movement.
o Civil Disobedience Movement (Dandi March).
o Quit India Movement ( 1942) .
o Round Table Conference and Poona Pact.
 Moderate and extremist phase ( 1885–1915):
o Establishment of Congress ( 1885) and important sessions (President and decisions).
o Partition of Bengal ( 1905) and the Swadeshi movement.
o Formation of Muslim League ( 1906) and Surat Split ( 1907) .
o Lucknow Pact ( 1916) and Home Rule League Movement.
 Revolutionary Activities:
o Bhagat Singh , Chandrashekhar Azad , Sukhdev , Rajguru ( HRA/HSRA) .
o Subhash Chandra Bose and the Indian National Army ( INA) .
o Kakori incident , Chittagong armoury raid.
2. Socio -Religious Reform Movements
from here often Founder , name of the organization and their objectives Are asked.
 Raja Rammohan Roy ( Brahmo Samaj , abolition of Sati).
 Dayanand Saraswati ( Arya Samaj , ' Return to the Vedas ') .
 Swami Vivekananda ( Ramakrishna Mission).
 Jyotiba Phule ( Satyashodhak Samaj) , Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar (widow remarriage).
 Theosophical Society and Annie Besant.
3. Governor Generals & Viceroys
The question often comes up in exams: "During the tenure of which Viceroy did such and such
incident take place ?"
 Lord Dalhousie: Doctrine of Lapse, Railways , Telegraph.
 Lord Wellesley: Subsidiary Alliance .
 Lord Curzon: Partition of Bengal.
 Lord Mountbatten: Partition and independence of India.
 Lord William Bentinck: Abolition of Sati practice.
 Lord Canning: The Revolution of 1857 and the First Viceroy.
